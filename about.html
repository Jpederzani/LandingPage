    About Me:

    Introduction

    Hello everyone, my name is Jonathan Pederzani and I attend Judson University in Elgin, IL. I am married with two kids, a four year old daughter and a two month old son, and we moved up here from Florida in December of 2019. I've spent most of my life in Florida and never had the chance to experience real seasons, and while I do miss warmth and humidity, I have come to appreicate the natural beauty of this state and its wilderness. I am a Computer Science major at Judson University with a minor in mathematics.


    What I hope to learn in this course

    In this course I hope to learn how to develop and engineer applications that are easily extensible and upgradable. Understanding how to utilize the internet, it's global reach, and existing infrastructure to produce applications that are current, viable, and efficient. Learning how to manage the process of not getting bogged down in details, being flexible and able to pivot onto a new items as they arise, and staying focused on the overall goal despite short-term failings.

    All of these things are important to me, from what I experienced as a technician at a small software company, has shown me the the fundemental principles of computer programming combined with the ability to recognize emergent technologies, capabilities, and trends to see where the industry is heading or should be. These items are often overlooked by students because we want to learn how to program before we learn about efficiently and correctly programming. However, is not one of the main tenants of software engineering and computer programming to use already existing work? To benefit from the hard work of others so that we can spend more time innovating and creating instead of re-inventing the wheel?
